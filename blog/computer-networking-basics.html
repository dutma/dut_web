Understanding Computer Networking: The Foundation of Modern Digital Communication

By Dut Dut | Networking | 4 min read

Introduction In today's interconnected world, computer networking forms the backbone of digital communication. Whether sending an email, accessing a website, streaming a video, or securing organizational systems, computer networks enable devices to exchange information efficiently and securely. As organizations increasingly depend on digital technologies, understanding networking concepts has become essential for IT professionals, data analysts, data scientists, and cybersecurity specialists.

What is Computer Networking?

Computer networking is the practice of connecting computers, servers, mobile devices, and other hardware to share data and resources. A network allows devices to communicate using standardized protocols and transmission methods. Examples include: .The Internet .Corporate office networks .University networks .Cloud computing environments .Mobile communication networks

Types of Computer Networks

1. Local Area Network (LAN) A LAN connects devices within a limited geographic area such as: .Offices .Schools .Homes LANs provide high-speed communication and resource sharing among connected devices. 2. Wide Area Network (WAN) A WAN connects multiple LANs across large geographic regions. Examples include: The Internet International corporate networks WANs rely on telecommunications infrastructure to facilitate communication over long distances. 3. Metropolitan Area Network (MAN) A MAN covers a city or metropolitan region and connects multiple local networks. 4. Wireless Local Area Network (WLAN) A WLAN enables communication through wireless technologies such as Wi-Fi, eliminating the need for physical cables.
